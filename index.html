Day 1 Survival Guide

Unlocked & Senior Approved ✅

Welcome to Delhi University! Explore the tabs below to read honest, senior-approved guides on preparing your documents, orientation fests, and selecting NEP courses.

📋 First Day Verification Blueprint: You must bring all original certificates along with 2 self-attested photocopies of each. Check off items as you gather them!
  • CSAS Allocation Form Printout (from CSAS Portal)
  • Class X Marksheet & Certificate (Date of Birth Proof)
  • Class XII Marksheet & Passing Certificate
  • CUET Scorecard Printout & Admit Card
  • Admission Fee Receipt Printout (Mandatory for college entry!)
  • Character Certificate (Issued within the last 6 months)
  • Category Certificate (SC/ST/OBC-NCL/EWS from current fiscal year)
  • Migration Certificate (For non-CBSE / out-of-Delhi board students)
  • 4–6 Passport Size Photos (Keep ready for library / bus card registration)
📄
Essential

Admission Fee Receipt Printout

Since your official college ID card will take weeks to be issued, college guards will only permit entry if you display your printed fee receipt. Keep it in your bag!

📓
Stationary

Notebook & Pen

Professors will write down syllabus layouts, list out recommended books, and detail the options for GE, SEC, and VAC papers in the first week. Take notes!

💧
Survival

Water Bottle & Umbrella

Delhi's admission and semester starting weather (July–August) is notoriously humid and rainy. Stay hydrated and carry a compact umbrella.

👕
Tips

Comfortable Clothing

You will be walking around departments, library blocks, and registration desks. Wear comfortable sneakers and light clothes.

🏫
Orientation Hall

Auditorium & Principal's Welcome

The Principal will address the freshers, introduce key teachers, and lay out general rules. Attendance isn't strictly graded, but attending is highly recommended to feel welcomed.

🎭
Society Expo

Audition registrations

Foyers and lawns will be lined with society tables (Dramatics, Music, Debate, Placement, Enactus). Ask questions, register for audition groups, and network early!

🤝
Mentorship

Department Tours

Seniors and teachers will guide you through lecture blocks, canteen hubs, and lab setups. Meet your classmates and save your class coordinator's number.

💡 DU NEP UGCF Syllabus System: Under the Undergraduate Curriculum Framework, you study more than just your main subject. Learn your subject categories:
📚
DSC (Core Courses)

Discipline Specific Core

These are your main subjects (e.g. History Hons, Physics Hons). You cannot change them and they form the base of your degree grades.

🌱
GE (Electives)

Generic Electives

Subjects you choose outside your department (e.g., studying Philosophy alongside Economics). Pick a GE that is scoring or lines up with future PG options.

🛠️
SEC (Skills)

Skill Enhancement Course

Practical classes (e.g., Creative Writing, Finance for Everyone, Python Programming). Pick a subject that adds weight to your placement CV!

❤️
VAC (Values)

Value Addition Course

Ethics and civic studies classes (e.g., Constitutional Values, Fit India, Vedic Mathematics). Generally lighter workloads but mandatory credits.

📋 DU Attendance Score Weightage (Out of 5 Marks):
• 85% and above = 5 Marks
• 80% to 84.9% = 4 Marks
• 75% to 79.9% = 3 Marks
• 70% to 74.9% = 2 Marks
• 67% to 69.9% = 1 Mark (Barely eligible for exams)
