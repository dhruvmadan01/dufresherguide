01
Welcome to Delhi
The Honest Truth

Congrats on surviving CUET — that was lowkey one of the hardest things you've done. Whether you landed North Campus, South Campus, or an off-campus college, you did that. Now brace yourself, because the first few weeks in Delhi will hit different.

"Delhi doesn't ease you in. It just drops you in the deep end and watches."

— Every DU third-year, ever

The blistering summer heat. Aggressive auto-wallahs. Metro lines that feel like a maze. PG brokers eyeing your naivety. It's a lot. This guide is your digital senior — the one who tells you where to eat for ₹50, how to dodge thousand-rupee scams, and how to engineer a legendary college life without destroying your bank account.

What to expect, no filter
  • 1The heat in May-June is not a vibe. Get a ceiling fan in your PG before anything else.
  • 2Homesickness is real. Plan a call home on a fixed day each week — helps more than you think.
  • 3The first month will feel like you're always lost. You're not. Everyone feels this way.
  • 4DU's campus culture is absolutely unmatched. Give it three weeks and you'll never want to leave.

Admissions
CSAS Playbook
Golden Rule

Always prioritize Course over College. A subject you hate from a brand-name college will ruin your GPA, your career, and your vibe.

Document Checklist — Bring All Three

Missing even one document at verification freezes your admission instantly. Carry 3 self-attested copies of everything.

  • ✓Class 10th & 12th Marksheets — Originals + DigiLocker verified copies
  • ✓CUET (UG) Scorecard + original Admit Card
  • ✓Category Certificate (OBC-NCL / EWS / SC / ST)
  • ✓4 passport-sized photos — white background only, no exceptions
  • ✓Aadhaar Card — physical + digital copy
⚠️

OBC/EWS certificate trap: It must be issued after March 31st of the current admission year. An older certificate gets auto-rejected by the portal with zero room for appeal. Verify the date before you leave home.

03
Accommodation
PG Hunting Guide

Finding accommodation in Delhi is a competitive sport. Brokers know you're young and desperate. They will try to charge you half a month's rent as "brokerage" for doing practically nothing.

North Campus Zones
  • 💛Vijay Nagar — most affordable, legendary street food, highest student density
  • 💛Hudson Lane — café central, slightly pricier but worth it for the vibe
  • 💛Malka Ganj / Indra Vihar — cheapest options if budget is super tight
South Campus Zones
  • 🩷Satya Niketan — heartbeat of South Campus, directly opposite Venky
  • 🩷Anand Niketan — quieter, safer, slightly premium
  • 🩷Moti Bagh — affordable alternative with decent connectivity

3 Red Flags — Spot These Before Signing Anything

  • 🚩Sub-Meter Electricity Scam: Low base rent + ₹10–12 per unit private sub-meter = your electricity bill will be shocking (literally). Always ask to see the official BSES rate.
  • 🚩Zero Ventilation: Rooms with no direct windows become concrete ovens. Your AC bill will destroy your budget and the air will make you miserable.
  • 🚩Vague Deposit Refund: Never accept verbal promises. Get it written on a ₹100 stamp paper — security deposit refunded within 7 days of vacating. Non-negotiable.

Pro Transit Hacks

  • 💡Get a Smart Card immediately — ₹200 upfront (includes ₹150 balance). Gives you 10% off every ride + extra 10% during off-peak hours. Tokens are a waste of time.
  • 💡Use official DMRC Travel app — QR tickets, real-time interchange info, no janky third-party apps.
  • 💡Auto bargaining formula: Check Rapido/Ola Bike price first. Use that screen price to negotiate with e-rickshaws. Works every single time.
  • 💡Shared e-rickshaws = ₹10/ride for short campus distances. Never pay auto rates for last-mile commutes.

Interview Hacks
The Brutal Truth No One Tells You

Top college societies don't hire the smartest or most arrogant student in the room. They hire the dependable one who will cheerfully print posters, clean up rooms, and do grunt work without throwing tantrums.

The 3-Step Interview Framework

  • 1
    The Availability Pitch
    Don't say "I'll balance it perfectly." Say: "I proactively track my syllabus weekly. During fest season, I'm ready to stay back late because my academics are always planned in advance."
  • 2
    Execution Over Titles
    Don't say "I was Head Boy." Say: "I personally coordinated seating, audio vendors, and scheduling for 500 external participants at our school fest." Prove you understand real work.
  • 3
    The No-Ego Close
    End by explicitly saying you're eager to learn from seniors and ready to execute any task to make the upcoming fest a massive success. No ego = instant respect.

Academics
Syllabus & The 10-Year Formula
The secret weapon

DU professors love repetition. 70–80% of exam questions are directly repeated or lightly rephrased from the last 5–10 years of question papers. Master those and you're sitting on a 8+ CGPA.

Don't spend your entire semester highlighting massive textbooks. Walk to the nearest campus xerox shop and ask for the "10-Year Question Bank" — usually published by Satish & Brothers or Shiv Das. It costs less than ₹100 and is worth more than any textbook.

The Attendance Trap

  • 📋Official Rule: Delhi University requires minimum 67% attendance to sit for semester exams. This is not a suggestion.
  • ⚠️Top colleges are strict: LSR, Hindu, Miranda House will detain you and bar you from the exam hall with zero exceptions. Don't gamble your entire year for a few extra hours in bed.
  • ✅Smart Move: Track your attendance weekly in a notes app. Know exactly how many classes you can skip before crossing the 67% line.
CGPA Matters for Placements

If your GPA drops below a threshold, major companies will bar you from interviews in final year. Don't let three years of work get blocked by low grades in semester 2.

Navigation
Metro Map Mastery

You need to understand three key interchange stations. Master these and you can get anywhere in Delhi without Google Maps.

1. Rajiv Chowk — Yellow + Blue Line

The busiest transit hub in Delhi. Use this when traveling from North Campus to Central Delhi (Connaught Place), or connecting towards Noida and Anand Vihar. You will be here often.

2. Kashmere Gate — Red + Yellow + Violet Line

Near North Campus. This is your gateway for catching interstate buses from the ISBT terminal, or traveling deep into East and West Delhi. Also useful for Agra/Lucknow bus routes home.

3. INA — Yellow + Pink Line

The critical link. Lets you move from North Campus Yellow Line to South Campus Pink Line in minutes. Also near Sarojini Nagar market — you will visit this station more than you expect.

Shopping
Sasta Shopping Guide

Setting up your PG room and building a college wardrobe doesn't need a credit card. Delhi's street markets are legendary — you just need the cheat sheet.

Wardrobe & Fashion

Sarojini Nagar Market — Pink Line

The mecca of budget fashion. Trendy tees, jeans, sneakers for ₹100–350. The Golden Bargaining Rule: offer 50% of whatever they quote and start walking away. They will call you back. Every. Single. Time.

Janpath Market — Near Connaught Place

Silver jewelry, aesthetic tote bags, vintage graphic tees. Organized and popular among students who want to upgrade their campus look without paying mall prices.

PG Setup & Room Decor

  • 🛏️Kamla Nagar Lanes (North Campus): Cheap buckets, hangers, bedsheets, cushions, study lamps — all at rock-bottom rates from street vendors.
  • 🛋️Lajpat Nagar Central Market: Curtains, rugs, minor furniture. If you want your shared flat to feel like an actual home, start here.
  • 📚Nai Sarak, Old Delhi: Best place in the city for second-hand textbooks, photocopied notes, stationery. Up to 70% cheaper than campus bookshops.

Health
Medical & Wellness Guide

Moving cities, eating tiffin food daily, and dealing with academic pressure will take a toll. Don't ignore your body or your mind.

WUS Health Centre — Free for DU Students

Located on both North and South campuses. Apply for a WUS health card for a negligible annual fee. You get access to qualified doctors, basic dental checkups, and subsidized or free medicines. Use it.

Government Hospitals — Zero Cost

For major emergencies, AIIMS and Safdarjung Hospital provide world-class treatment at practically zero cost. Save these addresses in Google Maps right now.

Staying Healthy in a PG

  • 💧Water Safety: Never drink Delhi tap water directly. Ensure your PG has a functioning RO purifier. If the water tastes odd, report it to your landlord immediately — water-borne infections in a new city are brutal.
  • 🍎Fruit Over Fried Food: When evening hunger hits, grab a ₹30 plate of seasonal fruit chaat from a street vendor instead of momos or fried rolls — at least 3 times a week. Your energy levels will actually thank you.
  • 🧠Mental Health Check: Missing home, feeling overwhelmed, anxiety about grades — all normal. Your college counselor and student welfare office exist for exactly this. Use them without shame.

Balance
Co-Curriculars vs Academics

The most common trap for high-achieving freshers: trying to join everything, burning out, and failing both academics and societies. Here's the math.

❌ The Burnout Path
  • → Join 5 societies
  • → Attendance drops
  • → CGPA tanks below 7
  • → Barred from placements
✓ The Strategic Path
  • → 1 core society
  • → 75%+ attendance
  • → 8+ CGPA
  • → High-paying corporate job
The Rule of One

Pick exactly ONE high-intensity society aligned with your career goal or your deepest passion. Give it everything.

You can join other clubs as a passive member. But don't take active organizing roles in multiple places. Your first priority is staying above 67% attendance and keeping your assignment grades solid.

DU Slang Decoded

Walk into DU on Day 1 and you'll hear words that aren't in any dictionary. Here's your cheat sheet so you don't look like a total fresher for too long.

Venky
Sri Venkateswara College, South Campus. If someone says "meet me near Venky," you now know.
Biryani Pool
Pooling money (usually ₹50–₹80 each) for a bulk biryani order at the end of a long study night. A sacred ritual.
Detain ho jaana
Getting barred from exams due to low attendance. The most feared phrase in any DU student's life. Avoid at all costs.
Xerox wala
The photocopy shop near college — your most important vendor. Buys you notes, prints assignments, laminate IDs. Know his face by Week 1.
OAT
Open Air Theatre — the central performance stage at North Campus. Concerts, fests, poetry slams. Your social life lives here.
Society Grind
The chaotic, exhausting, rewarding process of applying, interviewing, and working across college societies. Everyone goes through it.
Nai Sarak
Book market in Old Delhi. Where you buy second-hand textbooks for 70% less. A must-visit every semester start.
The Ridge
Delhi University's historic forest area near North Campus colleges. Green, peaceful, and full of monkeys who will steal your lunch.
Fresher's Party
The official welcome event organized by your college or society. Your first real social event. Go. Talk to people. Don't stand in corners.
Chole-Bhature Debrief
Informal post-exam post-mortem at Chache Di Hatti. Students discuss paper difficulty while stress-eating. A DU tradition.
Tiffin Bhai
Your local tiffin delivery guy. Treat him with respect. He holds immense power over your daily survival.
Proctor
The college official responsible for student discipline. Not your enemy — report ragging, harassment, or safety issues directly to them.
